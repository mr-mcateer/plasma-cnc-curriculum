‚õî THE #1 RULE ‚Äî READ THIS FIRST
SheetCam applies kerf compensation automatically. Your DXF should be drawn at exact final size. When you select the correct tool for your gauge, SheetCam offsets every toolpath by half the kerf width. If you already pre-compensated in SolidWorks, your part will be the wrong size. Do not double-compensate.

IMPORT YOUR DXF

SHEETCAM
1.1
File ‚Üí Import Drawing
Navigate to your USB drive. Select your LastF_ProjectName.dxf file. If prompted about units, choose Inches.
1.2
Check Scale Immediately
Hover over a known dimension on your drawing and read the coordinates at the bottom of the screen. If your 12" wide sign shows as 6" ‚Äî your DXF export scale was wrong. Go back to your workstation and re-export. Do not scale in SheetCam.
1.3
Check for Red Outlines (Islands)
Red contours = floating pieces with no bridge. These will fall into the water table. If you see red outlines on features like letter interiors (O, A, D, B, R, P, Q), eyes, or enclosed shapes ‚Äî stop. Go back to your workstation and add bridges in SolidWorks.
‚ö† IMPORT SETTINGS ‚Äî CHECK ONCE, CUT FOREVER
Go to Options ‚Üí Application Options ‚Üí Drawing Import. Verify: Arc fitting tolerance = 0.001", Smooth imported contours = checked, Remove zero-length segments = checked. These settings smooth jagged autotrace geometry without visibly changing your design. Set once, they persist across sessions.

3

CREATE A CONTOUR OPERATION

SHEETCAM

This tells SheetCam how to cut your design ‚Äî which tool, which direction, and how to approach each cut.

3.1
Operations ‚Üí New Contour Operation
Or click the blue contour icon in the toolbar. This opens the operation dialog.
3.2
Select Your Tool
In the Tool dropdown, pick the gauge-specific tool you set up (e.g., PM45-14ga-45A). All your feed rate, kerf, pierce values auto-populate from the tool.
3.3
Set Offset Direction
Outside offset for perimeter/outside cuts (torch cuts outside the line). Inside offset for holes and interior cutouts (torch cuts inside the line). No offset only for engraving/marking ‚Äî never for cutting.
‚õî THE OFFSET MISTAKE THAT RUINS PARTS
If your outside profile is set to Inside offset, or your holes are set to Outside offset, SheetCam compensates in the wrong direction ‚Äî your part shrinks or grows by the full kerf width. Always verify: Outside contours = Outside offset. Interior cuts = Inside offset.
3.4
Configure Lead-Ins
Set lead-in type to Arc (preferred) at 0.125" radius. Arc lead-ins create a smooth entry that reduces divot marks on the finished edge. For complicated designs with tight spaces, Line lead-in at 0.1" works as a fallback. Lead-in should start on scrap side, not on your finished edge.
3.5
Set Layer (if needed)
If your DXF has multiple layers, assign this operation to the correct layer. Tip: Cut interior features first, then outside profiles last. Create separate operations if needed ‚Äî inside cuts on one layer, outside on another.
3.6
Enable "Loop Sharp Corners" for Complicated Designs
In the Contour operation dialog, check Loop sharp corners. The plasma arc lags behind the torch ‚Äî without loops, sharp corners get rounded. The loop adds a tiny circular overcut at each corner to compensate. Essential for detailed signs and intricate silhouettes.

‚ö† SMALL HOLES ‚Äî THE #1 QUALITY KILLER
Holes smaller than ~1.5√ó material thickness cut poorly at full speed. For complicated designs with many small interior cutouts: create a separate operation for small features, reduce feed rate to 60% of normal, and set a 0.2" overcut to ensure the arc fully closes the loop. In SheetCam, you can also apply a Path Rule that automatically slows the torch for features below a size threshold.

CHECK CONSUMABLES BEFORE EVERY SESSION

POWERMAX45

Worn consumables cause bad cut quality, excessive dross, and failed pierces ‚Äî no amount of software tweaking fixes worn parts.

‚¨°
Nozzle
Replace when hole is oval, oversized, or has gouges. Most common wear part.
‚¨¢
Electrode
Replace when hafnium pit depth exceeds 0.040" (approximately 1mm). Deeper = misfires.
‚óé
Swirl Ring
Check for cracks or melted holes. Replace if air pattern is disrupted.
‚ä°
Shield
Inspect for buildup/damage. Dirty shield = poor arc stability. Clean or replace.
üîß Consumable rule: Use 45A shielded consumables for 12, 14, and 16 gauge mild steel. Set Powermax45 amperage dial to 45A. Match amps to consumable rating ‚Äî always.
6

HOME, ZERO, AND LOAD YOUR PROGRAM

COMMANDCNC
6.1
Power On & Release E-Stop
Turn on CNC controller and plasma station PC. Press the green arm button on the Blade Runner box. Release E-stop in CommandCNC UI. Verify no axis alarms on screen.
6.2
Home All Axes
Click Home All (or Home X, Home Y, Home Z individually). Wait until each axis shows Homed on screen. This establishes the machine's internal reference ‚Äî it is not your part zero.
6.3
Set Torch Disable = ON
CRITICAL: Toggle Torch Disable ON before loading any program. This prevents the torch from firing during jogging or dry runs. Only turn Torch Disable OFF when you are ready to actually cut.
6.4
Load Your G-Code File
File ‚Üí Open and navigate to USB. Select your .tap file. The program will display in the preview window. Verify the shape looks correct and fits within the cutting area.
6.5
Jog to Program Origin & Zero X/Y
Use the jog controls to move the torch to the lower-left corner of where you want the cut on your material. Click Zero X and Zero Y (or Touch Off ‚Üí X=0, Y=0). DROs should now read near X 0.000, Y 0.000. Do NOT manually zero Z ‚Äî the IHS (Initial Height Sense) probe handles Z automatically at each pierce.
‚úÖ ZEROING = WHERE YOUR PART STARTS ON THE SHEET
Homing (Step 6.2) tells the machine where it is. Zeroing (Step 6.5) tells the machine where your part is. They are completely different operations. You home once on startup. You re-zero for every new part position.
7

DRY RUN ‚Äî ALWAYS DO THIS

COMMANDCNC
7.1
Confirm Torch Disable is Still ON
Double check. If you skip this, the torch fires into empty air ‚Äî wasting consumables and creating a safety hazard.
7.2
Press Cycle Start
The gantry will trace the entire program without firing. Watch for: lead-in positions, cut order (insides first), travel clearance from material edges, and that the whole program fits on your sheet. If anything is wrong ‚Äî Feed Hold ‚Üí Stop ‚Äî re-zero or fix in SheetCam.

8

EXECUTE THE CUT

COMMANDCNC
8.1
Turn Torch Disable OFF
This is the only time you turn it off. Everyone must be clear of the table. Welding shade available. You are about to make plasma.
8.2
Verify THC is Enabled (Auto Mode)
On the CommandCNC screen, confirm THC = Auto/Enabled. THC (Torch Height Control) reads arc voltage during the cut and adjusts Z height to maintain the correct cut height from your SheetCam tool settings. Without THC, warped or uneven material causes inconsistent cut quality.
8.3
Press Cycle Start ‚Äî Monitor the Cut
The machine executes your program. Watch actively ‚Äî keep one hand near the keyboard for Feed Hold or E-stop. Look for: clean arc, minimal sparks topside, steady cutting sound, no torch dragging or diving.

Feed Rate Override¬± adjust speed on the fly (rotary knob or screen)
THC Voltage¬± adjust target voltage (raise = lower torch, lower = higher torch)
Feed HoldPauses cut. Arc extinguishes. Resume with Cycle Start.

PRE-CUT CHECKLIST ‚Äî COMPLETE AT THE STATION

Air pressure: 85+ PSI on compressor, Powermax reads 60 PSI cut pressure
Consumables: Nozzle and electrode inspected, not worn past limits
Powermax amperage: Dial set to 45A to match 45A consumables
Water table: Filled to slat level, material is above water
Ground clamp: Attached to bare metal on the workpiece (not slats, not paint)
Material secured: Flat on slats, not rocking or bridging gaps
Post processor: SheetCam used CommandCNC post
Correct tool: Selected the right gauge tool in SheetCam
Offset direction: Outside contours = outside, interior = inside
Toolpaths verified: Simulated in SheetCam, arrows correct direction
Homed: All axes homed in CommandCNC
Zero set: X and Y zeroed at program start on material
Dry run: Complete program traced with Torch Disable ON
THC: Set to Auto/Enabled
Safety: Glasses on, shade nearby, area clear, E-stop accessible
POP: Plan of Procedure is filled out and approved
‚úÖ AFTER THE CUT
Turn Torch Disable back ON. Let the piece cool before handling. Remove from table, clean dross with flap disk or wire wheel, and verify dimensions with calipers against your SolidWorks drawing. If dimensions are off by exactly one kerf width ‚Äî you had the wrong offset direction. Log your results for next time.
