Step 1 — AI Prompt for Plasma-Ready Images

Use this prompt structure
"Create a high-contrast black and white silhouette of [subject]. Solid black shapes on pure white background. No gradients, no gray tones, no shading. All interior details must be connected to the outer border with visible bridges. Design it as a single continuous stencil — no floating pieces. Clean vector-style edges, minimum detail width 3mm."
Key phrase: "single continuous stencil" tells the AI to connect islands. You'll still verify in SolidWorks, but this gets you 80% there.

Step 2 — SolidWorks Autotrace

Enable Add-In: Tools → Add-Ins → check Autotrace
New Part → open sketch on Front Plane
Insert image: Tools → Sketch Tools → Sketch Picture → select your PNG
Scale image to your desired final cut size now (not later)
Click the → arrow in Sketch Picture panel to enter Autotrace mode
Select area with rectangle tool → click Begin Trace
Adjust Color Tolerance ↑ and Recognition Tolerance ↓ until outlines are clean
Click Apply → repeat for any missed areas → OK
Pro tip: High-contrast B&W = better trace. If the AI image has gray anti-aliasing, bump up Color Tolerance to capture edges. Minimum 300 DPI for clean results.

Step 3 — Fix Floating Islands (The #1 Fail Point)

An "island" is any enclosed interior shape not connected to the main body. When plasma cuts it, the island falls out. Letters like A B D O P Q R all have islands. So do eyes, nostrils, windows, and any interior detail.

✗ No Bridges — Islands Fall Out

A B D O
Interior pieces have nothing connecting them to the frame. They drop into the water table.

✓ Bridged — Islands Stay Connected

A B D O
Small tabs/bridges connect each interior shape to the outer body. Nothing falls.
How to Add Bridges in SolidWorks
  • Zoom into each island → draw a small rectangle spanning the gap
  • Bridge width: ≥ material thickness (minimum 1/16" for 16ga, 3/32" for 12ga)
  • Use Trim Entities tool to merge the bridge into the path
  • Place bridges where they look intentional — tuck into natural shapes
  • 2 bridges per island for structural safety on larger pieces

45A Kerf & Minimum Size Reference

Gauge Thickness Kerf Width Min Feature Min Bridge
16 GA 0.060" 0.039" 0.080" 0.063" (1/16")
14 GA 0.075" 0.043" 0.090" 0.075" (5/64")
12 GA 0.105" 0.049" 0.100" 0.105" (3/32")
Heat Affected Zone (HAZ): Plasma heats ~0.02–0.04" beyond the kerf. Features narrower than 2× kerf width will warp, discolor, or melt through. That's your Min Feature column.
Rule of thumb: Nothing thinner than a nickel's edge (~0.077"). If you can't fit a nickel in the gap, it's too thin to survive the cut.

Step 4 — Export DXF from SolidWorks

Delete the sketch picture (image). Only sketch geometry should remain.
Right-click face → Export to DXF/DWG
Set version to R12 · Check "Splines as Polylines"
Check "High Quality DWG" + "Merge Points" · Scale 1:1
Save to USB with proper filename: LastF_ProjectName.dxf

Steps 5–6 — SheetCam → Cut

Open DXF in SheetCam → verify dimensions by measuring a known feature
Check for red outlines = pieces that will fall out. Go back and bridge them.
Select cut tool for your gauge → SheetCam applies kerf offset automatically
Set lead-in type (arc preferred) → verify toolpath direction (CW outside, CCW inside)
Run Post Processor → save as .ngc → load into CommandCNC → cut
SheetCam handles kerf for you. Do NOT pre-compensate for kerf in SolidWorks. Draw your design at exact final size — SheetCam offsets the toolpath by half the kerf width.

Pre-Plasma Checklist

  • No floating islands — every interior shape is bridged
  • All detail features are wider than 2× kerf for your gauge
  • Bridges are ≥ material thickness wide
  • Image deleted from sketch — only geometry remains
  • Exported as DXF R12 with splines as polylines
  • Filename follows: LastF_ProjectName.dxf
  • Measured a known dimension in SheetCam — scale is correct
  • No red outlines in SheetCam (nothing will fall out)
